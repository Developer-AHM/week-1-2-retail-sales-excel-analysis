# 📊 Week 1 - Retail Sales Excel Analysis

## 📖 Project Overview

This project was completed as part of the **LogicStack Data Analytics Internship – Week 1**. The objective was to analyze a retail sales dataset using **Google Sheets (Excel)** and **Python**. The project involved cleaning the dataset, validating data quality, performing basic analysis, creating charts, and documenting meaningful business insights.

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives

- Understand the structure of a retail sales dataset.
- Clean and organize the dataset for analysis.
- Perform data quality checks.
- Calculate key business metrics.
- Analyze sales by product category and gender.
- Create charts to visualize business insights.
- Summarize findings through data-driven observations.

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies

- Google Sheets (Excel)
- Python 3
- Jupyter Notebook
- Pandas
- GitHub
- Ubuntu Linux

---

## 📂 Dataset Information

The dataset contains **1,000 retail transactions** with the following columns:

- Transaction ID
- Date
- Customer ID
- Gender
- Age
- Product Category
- Quantity
- Price per Unit
- Total Amount

---

## ✅ Tasks Completed

### Part 1 – Dataset Understanding

- Explored the dataset structure.
- Identified rows, columns, and data types.

### Part 2 – Data Formatting

- Formatted the dataset.
- Adjusted column widths.
- Applied number and date formatting.
- Froze the header row.

### Part 3 – Data Quality Check

- Checked for blank cells.
- Checked for duplicate records.
- Verified Quantity values.
- Verified Price per Unit values.
- Validated Total Amount calculations.

### Part 4 – Basic Analysis

Calculated the following metrics:

- Total Sales
- Average Sale
- Minimum Sale
- Maximum Sale
- Total Quantity Sold
- Average Customer Age
- Youngest Customer
- Oldest Customer
- Total Transactions
- Unique Product Categories

### Part 5 – Sorting & Filtering

Performed sorting and filtering to:

- Identify highest and lowest transactions.
- Filter data by Product Category.
- Filter data by Gender.
- Filter transactions where Quantity > 2.
- Sort transactions by Total Amount.
- Sort customers by Age.

### Part 6 – Category-Based Analysis

- Total Sales by Product Category
- Total Quantity Sold by Product Category
- Total Sales by Gender

### Part 7 – Charts

Created the following charts:

- 📊 Bar Chart – Total Sales by Product Category
- 📈 Column Chart – Quantity Sold by Product Category
- 🥧 Pie Chart – Total Sales by Gender

### Part 8 – Observations

Documented meaningful observations based on the analysis.

---

## 📌 Key Observations

- Clothing products recorded the highest quantity sold, followed by Electronics and Beauty.
- Electronics generated the highest total sales among all product categories.
- Most customers in the dataset were female.
- A total of **2,514** products were sold across **1,000** transactions.
- Customer ages ranged from **18** to **64** years, with an average age of approximately **41** years.
- All data quality checks were completed successfully with no blank cells, duplicate records, or calculation errors.
