# Week 1 - Retail Sales Excel Analysis

## Project Overview

This project was completed as part of the **LogicStack Data Analytics Internship – Week 1**. The goal was to analyze a retail sales dataset using Google Sheets (Excel) and Python. The project involved cleaning and validating the data, performing basic sales analysis, creating charts, and documenting meaningful observations.

---

## Project Objective

The objective of this project was to:

* Understand the structure of a retail sales dataset.
* Clean and organize the data for analysis.
* Perform data quality checks.
* Calculate key business metrics.
* Analyze sales by product category and gender.
* Visualize insights using charts.
* Summarize the findings with data-driven observations.

---

* Google Sheets (Excel)
* Python 3

## Dataset Information

The dataset contains retail transaction records with the following columns:

* Transaction ID
* Date
* Customer ID
* Gender
* Age
* Product Category
* Quantity
* Price per Unit
* Total Amount

The dataset contains **1,000 retail transactions** used for analysis.

---

## Tasks Completed

### Part 1 – Dataset Understanding

* Explored the dataset structure.
* Identified rows, columns, and data types.

### Part 2 – Data Formatting

* Formatted the dataset as a table.
* Adjusted column widths.
* Applied number and date formatting.
* Froze the header row.

### Part 3 – Data Quality Check

* Checked for blank cells.
* Checked for duplicate records.
* Verified quantity values.
* Verified price values.
* Validated Total Amount calculations.

### Part 4 – Basic Analysis

* Calculated:

  * Total Sales
  * Average Sale
  * Minimum & Maximum Sale
  * Total Quantity Sold
  * Average Customer Age
  * Youngest & Oldest Customer
  * Total Transactions
  * Unique Product Categories

### Part 5 – Sorting & Filtering

* Identified highest and lowest transactions.
* Filtered data by:

  * Product Category
  * Gender
  * Quantity
* Sorted transactions by sales and customer age.

### Part 6 – Category-Based Analysis

* Calculated total sales by product category.
* Calculated quantity sold by product category.
* Calculated total sales by gender.

### Part 7 – Charts

Created:

* Bar Chart – Total Sales by Product Category
* Column Chart – Quantity Sold by Product Category
* Pie Chart – Total Sales by Gender

### Part 8 – Observations

Documented key insights obtained from the analysis.

---

## Key Observations

1. Clothing products had the highest quantity sold, followed by Electronics and then Beauty.
2. Most of the customers in the dataset were female.
3. Electronics generated the highest total sales, followed by Clothing and then Beauty.
4. A total of **2,514** products were sold across all transactions.
5. The youngest customer was **18** years old, while the oldest customer was **64** years old.
6. The average customer age was approximately **41 years**.
7. All data quality checks passed successfully, with no blank cells, duplicate records, or calculation errors found.
